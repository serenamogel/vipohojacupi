Master P Net Worth: Career &amp; Lifestyle

August 2024 · 11 minute read

Master P’s net worth is $200 million as an American rapper, actor, record producer, and business personality.

Master P was a part of hip hop group TRU through which he gained fame during the mid-90s era. Besides, he also had a successful solo career.

With the release of his fifth studio album, Ice Cream Man, his fame was in the stratosphere. The album contained his first single, “Mr. Ice Cream Man.

Similarly, in 1997 his popularity went even higher with the single “Make ‘Em Say Uhh!” certified platinum by RIAA.

Master P has released a total of 15 studio albums to date. Similarly, Master P also serves as the CEO of P. Miller Enterprises.

He also laid his hands on the media industry with his television company Better Black Television which did not perform well.

Apart from that, Master P is a founder of No Limit Records, later re-launched under the name New No Limit Records through Koch and Universal Records.

The record label has currently named No Limit Forever Records.

Besides music and business, Master P also embarked acting career. He starred in movies such as Soccer Mom, and I Got the Hook, and Foolish.

Similarly, he also has appeared in television series starring in the sitcom Romeo.

Early Life

Master P was born Percy Robert Miller on April 29, 1967, in New Orleans in the Calliope Projects.

Oldest of five siblings, he has one younger sister and three brothers. Two of his brothers are also rap artist Corey Miller with his stage name “C-Murder” and Vyshonne Miller with the stage name “Silkk the Shocker.”

Moreover, Master P enrolled in Booker T Washington High School and also attended Warren Easton High School.

He played high school basketball and, after graduation, went to the University of Houston on an athletic scholarship.

Nonetheless, he dropped out of the University of Houston and transferred to Merritt College in Oakland to major in business administration.

Master P later opened up a record store with the money that came from his grandfather’s death due to malpractice settlement. He named the store No Limit Records, which became the foundation for his own record label of the same name.
