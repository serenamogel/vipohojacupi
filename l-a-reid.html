Who is L.A. Reid?

L.A. Reid, birthed as Antonio Marquis Reid was born on June 7, 1956, in Cincinnati, Ohio. He was a music fanatic since he was a boy, listening to James Brown, Sly Stone, and Led Zeppelin. Reid was noted in his youth for converting virtually anything into a drum set—pots, pans, and whatever else would do. Cincinnati, with its own rich musical traditions, also had an important role in his musical growth. Reid was playing drums in a band in his teens, honing his skills in front of small crowds. Reid’s notoriety and persona were enhanced by his selective taste in luxury clothing, inspiring his friends to dub him “L.A.”

How many businesses does L.A. Reid own?

Reid is a record executive, A&R representative, and record producer from the United States. He founded Hitco Entertainment and served as its co-chairman. He previously held the positions of chairman and CEO of Epic Records and The Island Def Jam Music Group, as well as president and CEO of Arista Records.

He began his career as a drummer for the Ohio R&B bands Pure Essence in 1973 and The Deele (with future business partner Babyface) in 1981. Reid has also worked as a record producer, programmer, and instrumentalist for a number of recording artists.
