Kirstein Dunst Career

Acting Career

Dunst started her career at the young age of 6, where she debuted as a child actor in Woody Allen’s short movie Oedipus Wrecks.

In her teenage days, she starred in several movies like Wag the Dog, Ture Heart, Drop Dead Gorgeous, Bring it On, Gets Over it, etc.

Till now, she hadn’t been able to be featured in the commercially successful film, but soon after, she appeared in Spider-Man as the love interest of Peter Parker, Mary Jane.

The character of Mary Jane portrayed by Dunst was instantly adored by the fans, and she then became synonyms with the character of Mary Jane in future movies as well.

Kirstein has acted in many critically acclaimed movies like The Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind, Marie Antoinette, Elizabethtown, and Melancholia.

She won best actress awards in several film festivals for the latter.

Musical Career

Kirstein always loved to sing.

She even starred in the comedy movie Get Over It to take the opportunity to showcase her singing in the movie.

Later she started singing in on-screen as well as off-screen.

In the final installment of the Spider-Man franchise, i.e., Spider-Man 3, Dunst sang two songs like Mary Jane Watson.

Dunst also has appeared in music videos like Savage Garden’s “I Knew I Loved You,” Beastie Boys “Make Some Noise,” and R.E.M’s “We All Go Back to Where We Belong.”
