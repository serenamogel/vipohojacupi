David Cook | Career

At 13, David purchased a guitar and performed in musicals and choirs throughout his schooling.

Initially, he was into playing baseball, but he got into music after he suffered the injury.

Cook was very much active in music during his high school days.

He wrote his debut song for his band named Axium when he was merely 15 years old.

Success followed his band with recognition, such as the Best Kansas City band in 2004.

The band released three studio albums, but the singer joined another band named Midwest Kings and released a solo album titled Analog Heart.

Though he entered the American Idol competition in 2008 to support his brother, the singer came out as an eventual winner beating David Archuleta in the finale.

In 2001, the singer’s second major-label album, “This Loud Morning,” was released.

After leaving the RCA record, he released his first single, “Criminals,” for his fourth studio album Digital Vein, which was released in 2015.

Moreover, the album also produced two more singles, “Broken Heart” and “Heartbeat.”

To promote his first EP, Chromance, Cook released the lead single “Gimme Heartbreak.”

Moreover, his second EP, The Looking Glass, was released on April 16, 2021.
